1943 — The Origin
The First Neural Network Model

Warren McCulloch (neuroscientist) and Walter Pitts (logician) published "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity" — proposing that neurons could be modelled as simple binary logic gates. This paper is widely regarded as the founding document of both neural network theory and computational neuroscience.

Their model showed that networks of artificial neurons could compute any logical or arithmetic function — a concept that would evolve over 80 years into today's billion-parameter models.

1950
The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes "Can Machines Think?" introducing the imitation game — the first formal definition of machine intelligence.

1956
The Dartmouth Conference

McCarthy, Minsky, Shannon and 6 others coin the term "Artificial Intelligence" — officially founding it as a field of study.

1997
Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's chess computer becomes the first machine to beat a reigning world champion under standard tournament conditions.

2012
AlexNet & Deep Learning

Krizhevsky's CNN wins ImageNet by a landmark margin — igniting the modern deep learning revolution.
