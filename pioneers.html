Founding Era (1940s–1960s)
Founder · 1943
🧠
Warren McCulloch
1898 – 1969
Neuroscientist & Logician

Neuroscientist who collaborated with Walter Pitts to publish the 1943 paper that launched neural network theory — proposing that neurons could compute any logical function.

Key contributions
McCulloch–Pitts neuron model (1943)
Logical calculus of nervous activity
Theoretical foundation of ANNs
NeuralFounder
Founder · 1943
📐
Walter Pitts
1923 – 1969
Mathematician & Logician

Self-taught mathematical prodigy who co-authored the landmark 1943 neural network paper with Warren McCulloch at age 20. His formalism made the neuron model mathematically rigorous.

Key contributions
McCulloch–Pitts neuron (1943)
First mathematical model of brain
Binary threshold functions
NeuralFounder
Founder · 1950
🔐
Alan Turing
1912 – 1954
Mathematician & Computer Scientist

Father of theoretical computer science and AI. His 1950 paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" proposed the Turing Test — the first formal framework for machine intelligence — and predicted AI's development with startling accuracy.

Key contributions
Turing Test — imitation game (1950)
Universal Turing Machine (1936)
Codebreaking Enigma — WWII AI
Theory of morphogenesis & pattern formation
TheoryFounder
Founder · 1956
🧩
John McCarthy
1927 – 2011
Father of Artificial Intelligence

Coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" and organised the 1956 Dartmouth Conference. Invented Lisp (1958), a programming language that powered AI research for three decades. Pioneered time-sharing computing at MIT and Stanford.

Key contributions
Coined "Artificial Intelligence" (1956)
Dartmouth Conference organiser
Lisp programming language (1958)
Situation calculus & formal logic
SymbolicFounder
Founder · 1951
🤖
Marvin Minsky
1927 – 2016
Co-founder MIT AI Lab

Built SNARC (1951) — the first neural network machine. Co-founded the MIT AI Lab with McCarthy. His 1969 book "Perceptrons" nearly ended neural network research, yet he remained a central figure in AI philosophy and cognitive science.

Key contributions
SNARC — first neural network machine
Co-founded MIT AI Lab (1959)
Frames theory of knowledge
Society of Mind theory
SymbolicFounder
Founder · 1956
⚙️
Herbert Simon
1916 – 2001
Nobel Laureate · Cognitive Scientist

Nobel Prize in Economics (1978). With Allen Newell, built Logic Theorist (1956) and General Problem Solver (1957). His work bridged economics, psychology, and AI through bounded rationality and heuristic search.

Key contributions
Logic Theorist (1956)
General Problem Solver (1957)
Bounded rationality theory
Nobel Prize in Economics (1978)
SymbolicFounder
ML Pioneer · 1959
🎮
Arthur Samuel
1901 – 1990
Father of Machine Learning

IBM researcher who coined "Machine Learning" in 1959 while building a self-improving checkers program. His program became expert-level by studying its own games — the first demonstration of a learning machine improving beyond its programmer's ability.

Key contributions
Coined "Machine Learning" (1959)
Self-learning checkers program
Alpha-beta pruning concepts
MLFounder
Neural · 1957
👁️
Frank Rosenblatt
1928 – 1971
Inventor of the Perceptron

Created the Perceptron (1957) — the first algorithm capable of learning from data through supervised training. The US Navy funded it as a machine that could perceive and identify its surroundings. His tragic early death at 43 cut short a revolutionary career.

Key contributions
Perceptron — first learning algorithm
Mark I Perceptron hardware
Supervised learning foundations
NeuralFounder
Neural Renaissance (1970s–1990s)
Neural Pioneer
🏆
Geoffrey Hinton
b. 1947
Godfather of Deep Learning · Turing Award 2018

The single most influential figure in modern deep learning. Popularised backpropagation (1986), co-invented Boltzmann machines, Dropout, and Capsule Networks. His student Krizhevsky's AlexNet (2012) ignited the deep learning revolution. Left Google in 2023 to speak freely about AI risks.

Key contributions
Backpropagation (Rumelhart, Williams, 1986)
Deep Belief Networks (2006)
Dropout regularisation
Turing Award (2018) with LeCun & Bengio
NeuralML
Neural Pioneer
🌊
Yann LeCun
b. 1960
Father of CNNs · Turing Award 2018

Invented Convolutional Neural Networks (LeNet, 1989) while at Bell Labs, creating a system that read 10% of US cheques by the 1990s. His work at NYU and Meta (Chief AI Scientist) made CNNs the dominant computer vision architecture.

Key contributions
LeNet — Convolutional Neural Networks (1989)
MNIST dataset & benchmarking
Self-supervised learning advocacy
Turing Award (2018)
NeuralVision
Neural Pioneer
📊
Yoshua Bengio
b. 1964
Deep Learning Pioneer · Turing Award 2018

Professor at Université de Montréal whose lab pioneered deep learning, attention mechanisms, and generative models. Co-invented word embeddings (2001) and the attention mechanism foundational to Transformers. Strong AI safety advocate.

Key contributions
Word embeddings (neural language models)
Attention mechanisms for seq2seq
GAN theory contributions
Turing Award (2018)
NeuralNLP
RL Pioneer
🎯
Richard Sutton
b. 1956
Father of Reinforcement Learning

Developed temporal-difference learning (1988) and co-authored "Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction" — the definitive RL textbook. His "Bitter Lesson" essay (2019) argues that general computation always beats human knowledge in AI.

Key contributions
Temporal-Difference Learning (TD)
Policy gradient methods
RL textbook (with Barto)
"The Bitter Lesson" (2019)
RL
LSTM · 1997
🔄
Sepp Hochreiter
b. 1967
Inventor of LSTM

Co-invented Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) with Jürgen Schmidhuber in 1997 — a recurrent network that solved the vanishing gradient problem. LSTMs powered speech recognition, translation, and text generation for two decades before Transformers.

Key contributions
LSTM (1997) — memory for sequences
Vanishing gradient problem analysis
Gated recurrent architectures
NLPNeural
Vision · 2007
👁️
Fei-Fei Li
b. 1976
Creator of ImageNet

Stanford professor who built ImageNet (2007–2009) — the 14-million-image dataset that triggered the 2012 deep learning explosion. Pioneer of AI ethics and co-founded AI4ALL to promote diversity in AI education. Former Google Cloud Chief Scientist.

Key contributions
ImageNet dataset (2007)
ILSVRC competition (2010)
AI4ALL — diversity in AI
Spatial transformer networks
VisionEthics
The Modern Era (2010s–Present)
Transformer Era
💬
Ilya Sutskever
b. 1986
Co-founder OpenAI · AlexNet & GPT

Hinton's PhD student who co-invented AlexNet (2012) — the paper that started the deep learning revolution. Co-founded OpenAI and led GPT, GPT-2, GPT-3, and GPT-4 development. One of the most influential figures in the current AI wave.

Key contributions
AlexNet (2012)
Sequence-to-Sequence learning
GPT series — OpenAI
NeuralLLM
AlphaGo Era
♟️
Demis Hassabis
b. 1976
Co-founder DeepMind · Nobel Prize 2024

Child chess prodigy who co-founded DeepMind (2010, acquired by Google 2014). Led development of AlphaGo, AlphaFold, Gemini, and Gemma. Awarded the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for AlphaFold's protein structure predictions.

Key contributions
AlphaGo & AlphaZero (2016–2018)
AlphaFold 2 — protein folding (2021)
DQN — Atari from pixels (2013)
Nobel Prize Chemistry (2024)
RLScience
LLM Era
🌟
Ashish Vaswani
b. ~1985
Lead Author — "Attention Is All You Need"

Google Brain researcher who led the team that published "Attention Is All You Need" (2017) — the Transformer paper that underpins every modern LLM. This single paper, cited 100,000+ times, is the most consequential ML paper of the decade.

Key contributions
Transformer architecture (2017)
Multi-head self-attention
Positional encodings
LLMNLP
GPT Era
🤖
Sam Altman
b. 1985
CEO OpenAI · Former YC President

Former Y Combinator president who became CEO of OpenAI in 2019. Oversaw the development and launch of GPT-3, DALL-E, ChatGPT, and GPT-4 — transforming AI from a research field into the world's fastest-growing consumer technology.

Key contributions
ChatGPT launch strategy (2022)
OpenAI commercial scaling
GPT-4 & DALL-E oversight
LLMIndustry
AI Safety
🛡️
Dario Amodei
b. 1983
CEO Anthropic · Former OpenAI VP

Physicist and AI researcher who led GPT-2 and GPT-3 research at OpenAI before co-founding Anthropic (2021) with his sister Daniela to focus on AI safety. Developed Constitutional AI and Claude — an AI assistant built with safety-first principles.

Key contributions
Constitutional AI (CAI)
Claude AI assistant
RLHF scaling research
AI safety advocacy at scale
SafetyLLM
Generative AI
🎨
Ian Goodfellow
b. 1985
Inventor of GANs

Invented Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) in a single night in 2014 after a debate about neural networks in a Montreal bar. GANs launched the generative AI revolution — deepfakes, synthetic data, artistic generation, and eventually diffusion models.

Key contributions
GANs — Generative Adversarial Networks (2014)
Deep Learning textbook (Goodfellow, Bengio)
Virtual adversarial training
GenerativeVision
Robotics
🦿
Rodney Brooks
b. 1954
Father of Modern Robotics

MIT professor who challenged symbolic AI with "behaviour-based robotics" — arguing that intelligence emerges from simple reactive systems, not reasoning. Founded iRobot (Roomba) and Rethink Robotics. Pioneered the reactive/subsumption architecture.

Key contributions
Subsumption architecture (1986)
Co-founded iRobot — Roomba (2002)
Behaviour-based robotics paradigm
Robotics
Safety Theorist
📖
Nick Bostrom
b. 1973
AI Risk Philosopher

Oxford philosopher who founded the Future of Humanity Institute and wrote "Superintelligence" (2014) — the book that made AI existential risk a mainstream concern and influenced Elon Musk, Bill Gates, and the AI safety research agenda.

Key contributions
"Superintelligence" (2014)
Existential risk framework
Future of Humanity Institute
Safety
