Year by year

Complete AI Timeline

Every major event from McCulloch & Pitts' foundational 1943 paper through today's generative AI era.

40s

The Genesis Years

Mathematics & neuroscience merge to conceive the thinking machine

1940 – 1949
1943
McCulloch–Pitts Neuron

Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts publish "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity," creating the first mathematical model of a neuron. They demonstrate that networks of such neurons can compute any logical function.

Neural Foundational
1944
Hebbian Learning Theory

Donald Hebb's "The Organization of Behavior" introduces the principle "neurons that fire together, wire together" — the first learning rule for neural networks, still influential in modern neuroscience and unsupervised ML.

Neural Learning
1945
Von Neumann Architecture

John von Neumann's "First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC" describes the stored-program computer — the hardware foundation that would make software AI systems possible.

Hardware
1948
Cybernetics — Wiener

Norbert Wiener publishes "Cybernetics: Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine," establishing feedback systems theory that bridges biology and engineering — the forerunner of adaptive AI systems.

Theory Neural
50s

Turing's Vision & AI's Birth

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined; early programs beat humans at games

1950 – 1959
1950
Turing's "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"

Alan Turing proposes the Imitation Game (later "Turing Test") to define machine intelligence. He predicts that by 2000, machines will be able to fool 30% of human judges for 5 minutes. His paper frames decades of AI debate.

Foundational Theory
1951
SNARC — First Neural Network Computer

Marvin Minsky and Dean Edmonds build SNARC (Stochastic Neural Analog Reinforcement Calculator), the first artificial neural network machine, using 3000 vacuum tubes to simulate 40 neurons.

Neural Hardware
1956
Dartmouth Conference — AI is Born

John McCarthy organises the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence. Attendees include Claude Shannon, Marvin Minsky, and Nathaniel Rochester. "Artificial Intelligence" enters the lexicon as a formal discipline.

Milestone Symbolic
1956
Logic Theorist

Allen Newell, Herbert Simon, and Cliff Shaw's "Logic Theorist" proves 38 of 52 theorems from Principia Mathematica — often called the first true AI program. Simon predicts machines will surpass humans in every intellectual domain within 20 years.

Symbolic Reasoning
1957
The Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt introduces the Perceptron at Cornell, the first algorithm capable of learning from data. The US Navy funds it as a machine that can "perceive, recognize and identify its surroundings." Sparks the first neural network boom.

Neural ML
1958
Lisp Programming Language

John McCarthy invents Lisp (LISt Processing language), which becomes the dominant language for AI research for the next three decades. Its symbolic processing and recursive capabilities make it ideal for early AI programs.

Symbolic Tools
1959
Machine Learning Coined — Samuel

Arthur Samuel at IBM coins "Machine Learning" while building his famous checkers-playing program — one of the first self-learning programs, becoming world-class by studying millions of moves from human games.

ML Milestone
1959
General Problem Solver

Newell & Simon's GPS attempts to formalise human problem-solving using "means-ends analysis" — the first program with a general search strategy separable from domain knowledge.

Symbolic Reasoning
60s

First Successes & Early Limits

Natural language, computer vision, and robotics emerge — then meet hard walls

1960 – 1969
1961
Unimate — First Industrial Robot

General Motors installs Unimate on its assembly line in New Jersey — the first industrial robotic arm. It lifts hot metal castings from a die-casting machine, beginning the automation of physical labor.

Robotics
1964
ELIZA — First Chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT creates ELIZA, a program that simulates a Rogerian psychotherapist by pattern-matching user input. Users become emotionally attached despite it understanding nothing — raising early questions about anthropomorphism.

NLP Milestone
1965
DENDRAL — First Expert System

Feigenbaum, Lederberg, and Buchanan at Stanford build DENDRAL to identify molecular structures from mass spectrometry data. It outperforms human chemists — the first expert system to demonstrate superhuman domain performance.

Symbolic Expert System
1966
The Summer Vision Project

Seymour Papert assigns an MIT undergrad to "solve" computer vision in one summer. The field would take 50 more years to approach the goal — but this project formally founded Computer Vision as a research area.

Vision
1966
ALPAC Report — NLP Setback

The Automatic Language Processing Advisory Committee reports that machine translation is twice as expensive and half as accurate as human translation. US funding for NLP research collapses — the first hint of "AI Winter."

NLP Setback
1969
Minsky & Papert: Perceptrons

Minsky and Papert's book "Perceptrons" proves that single-layer perceptrons cannot learn XOR. Although multi-layer networks weren't ruled out, the book nearly killed neural network research for a decade.

Neural Setback
1969
Shakey — First Mobile AI Robot

SRI's Shakey becomes the first general-purpose mobile robot able to reason about its own actions. It combines perception, reasoning, and action — foreshadowing autonomous vehicles by 40 years.

Robotics Symbolic
70s

First AI Winter

Funding collapses; expert systems continue quietly in labs

1970 – 1979 · AI Winter I
1973
The Lighthill Report

Sir James Lighthill's report to the UK government concludes AI research has failed to deliver on its promises. The British government withdraws most AI funding, beginning a period of severe resource constraints across the field.

AI Winter I
1972
MYCIN Expert System

Stanford's MYCIN diagnoses blood infections and recommends antibiotics using a rule-based system. It matches expert physicians but is never used clinically — raising questions about AI accountability. Its backward-chaining reasoning design influenced all later expert systems.

Symbolic Expert System
1974
Backpropagation — Werbos

Paul Werbos's PhD thesis derives backpropagation for training multi-layer networks — arguably the most important algorithm in deep learning. Its significance goes unrecognised for another decade.

Neural ML
1976
PROLOG Programming Language

Alain Colmerauer and Robert Kowalski develop PROLOG, a logic-programming language that becomes the backbone of symbolic AI and expert systems research in the 1980s.

Symbolic Tools
1979
Stanford Cart

Hans Moravec's Stanford Cart successfully navigates a cluttered room autonomously — a significant demonstration of real-world computer vision and autonomous navigation, taking 5 hours to cross a 20-foot room.

Vision Robotics
80s

The Expert Systems Boom

Backpropagation resurfaces; neural networks rise again

1980 – 1989 · Expert Systems Era
1980
Expert Systems Go Commercial

XCON (R1) at Digital Equipment Corporation saves the company $40M per year by configuring computer orders. AI becomes a commercial industry — by 1985, Fortune 500 companies spend $1 billion annually on in-house AI departments.

Symbolic Expert System Commercial
1981
Japan's Fifth Generation Project

Japan's Ministry of International Trade and Industry funds a ¥50 billion 10-year plan to build intelligent computers using parallel logic processing. It triggers massive US and European AI investment programs.

Policy
1982
Hopfield Networks

John Hopfield introduces recurrent neural networks with associative memory properties. His paper re-legitimises neural network research and sparks a second wave of interest in learning machines.

Neural ML
1986
Backpropagation Popularised — Rumelhart & Hinton

Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams publish "Learning Representations by Back-propagating Errors" in Nature — the paper that makes multi-layer neural networks practical. It launches the modern era of neural network research.

Neural Milestone
1987
AI Winter II Begins

Desktop computers surpass Lisp machines on price-performance. Apple and IBM win the business market. $500M expert systems industry collapses. DARPA cuts AI funding. The second AI winter begins.

AI Winter II
1989
LeNet — Convolutional Neural Networks

Yann LeCun and team at Bell Labs create LeNet — the first practical convolutional neural network, trained with backprop to recognise handwritten zip codes for the US Postal Service. It reads 10% of US cheques by the late 1990s.

Neural Vision Milestone
90s

Statistical Methods Rise

Machine learning matures; Deep Blue defeats Kasparov; the Web creates data

1990 – 1999
1990
TD-Gammon — Reinforcement Learning

Gerald Tesauro's TD-Gammon learns to play backgammon at expert level using temporal-difference reinforcement learning with no prior knowledge — one of the first successful applications of RL to a complex domain.

RL ML
1992
Support Vector Machines

Vapnik and Cortes develop the SVM — a principled statistical method for classification that dominates ML competitions throughout the 2000s, before being supplanted by deep learning.

ML
1995
Random Forests & Boosting

Freund and Schapire introduce AdaBoost; Breiman later formalises Random Forests. Ensemble methods prove that combining weak learners creates powerful predictors — still widely used today.

ML
1997
Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov 3.5–2.5 in a six-game match — the first time a computer beats a reigning world champion under standard tournament conditions. A landmark AI cultural moment.

Milestone Symbolic RL
1997
LSTM — Long Short-Term Memory

Hochreiter and Schmidhuber publish LSTM — a recurrent neural network architecture that solves the vanishing gradient problem. LSTMs will power speech recognition, translation, and text generation for two decades.

Neural NLP
1998
PageRank & Web-Scale Data

Brin and Page's PageRank algorithm — foundation of Google — applies statistical ML to the web. The internet creates the first large-scale datasets that will fuel the coming data-driven AI revolution.

ML NLP
00s

Data Age & Real-World AI

Robots drive cars, spam filters deploy, ImageNet begins

2000 – 2009 · Data Age
2001
Bayesian Spam Filtering

Paul Graham's "A Plan for Spam" popularises Bayesian ML for email classification — the first time ML touched hundreds of millions of people's daily lives. Spam filtering becomes a mass-scale ML deployment.

ML NLP
2002
Roomba — Consumer Robotics

iRobot ships Roomba, the first mass-market autonomous robot — a vacuum cleaner with sensors and a reactive AI. Over 30 million units sold, making AI-powered robotics a household reality.

Robotics
2004
DARPA Grand Challenge

DARPA offers $1M for the first autonomous vehicle to complete a 142-mile desert course. No team finishes in 2004, but Stanford's Stanley wins in 2005 — launching the autonomous vehicle industry and spawning Waymo, Tesla AP, Cruise, and others.

Robotics Vision Milestone
2006
Deep Belief Networks — Hinton

Geoffrey Hinton and Simon Osindero publish "A Fast Learning Algorithm for Deep Belief Nets," showing that deep networks can be trained effectively using layer-by-layer pre-training. This paper reignites interest in deep learning.

Neural ML
2007
Fei-Fei Li Starts ImageNet

Stanford professor Fei-Fei Li conceives and begins building ImageNet — 14 million labelled images across 22,000 categories. The dataset that will trigger the deep learning revolution is quietly assembled over three years.

Vision Data
2008
GPU Computing for ML

NVIDIA's CUDA enables general-purpose GPU programming. Andrew Ng's group at Stanford demonstrates that GPUs can train deep networks 70× faster than CPUs — setting the hardware foundation for the coming deep learning explosion.

Hardware Neural
10s

The Deep Learning Revolution

AlexNet, AlphaGo, GANs, Transformers — AI surpasses humans domain by domain

2010 – 2019 · Deep Learning Era
2012
AlexNet Wins ImageNet — Deep Learning Ignites

Krizhevsky, Sutskever, and Hinton's AlexNet achieves 15.3% top-5 error on ImageNet — 10.9 percentage points better than the next best entry. The result is so stunning it causes the entire field to pivot to deep learning. Every tech giant begins a massive hiring spree for DL talent.

Neural Vision Milestone
2013
Word2Vec — Deep Representations for NLP

Mikolov et al. at Google publish Word2Vec — learning distributed word representations showing that word relationships encode semantic meaning ("king" − "man" + "woman" ≈ "queen"). It transforms NLP from feature engineering to representation learning.

NLP Neural
2014
GANs — Generative Adversarial Networks

Ian Goodfellow et al. introduce GANs — a framework of two competing networks (generator vs discriminator) that can produce photorealistic images. Yann LeCun calls it "the most interesting idea in the last 10 years of machine learning."

Neural Vision Generative
2014
Sequence-to-Sequence Learning

Sutskever, Vinyals, and Le publish Seq2Seq with attention — enabling neural machine translation. Google switches to neural MT in 2016, immediately improving translation quality more than in the previous 10 years combined.

NLP Neural
2016
AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol

DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Go world champion Lee Sedol 4–1. Go has more positions than atoms in the universe — its mastery was thought to be at least a decade away. Move 37 in Game 2 is described as "the most creative" move a human or machine has ever played in Go history.

RL Neural Milestone
2017
"Attention Is All You Need" — The Transformer

Vaswani et al. at Google Brain publish the Transformer architecture — replacing RNNs with self-attention. This paper becomes the single most impactful ML paper of the decade, directly leading to BERT, GPT, T5, and every modern LLM.

NLP Neural LLM Milestone
2018
BERT & Transfer Learning in NLP

Google's BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) achieves state-of-the-art on 11 NLP benchmarks. Transfer learning — pretrain on massive data, fine-tune on tasks — becomes the dominant NLP paradigm.

NLP LLM
2018
GPT-1 — OpenAI's First Language Model

OpenAI releases GPT (117M parameters) trained on BooksCorpus, demonstrating that unsupervised pre-training on large text data followed by task fine-tuning achieves strong results across diverse NLP tasks.

LLM NLP
2019
GPT-2 & "Too Dangerous to Release"

OpenAI's GPT-2 (1.5B params) generates coherent long-form text so convincingly they initially refuse to release the full model over "misuse concerns." The controversy sparks the AI safety and governance debate that dominates the 2020s.

LLM Safety
20s

The Generative AI Era

LLMs, multimodal models, and AI assistants reach billions of people

2020 – Present · LLM Era
2020
GPT-3 — 175B Parameters

OpenAI's GPT-3 (175 billion parameters) demonstrates emergent few-shot learning — solving tasks it was never explicitly trained on with just a few examples in the prompt. Microsoft licenses it exclusively for $1 billion.

LLM Milestone
2021
DALL-E & CLIP — Text-to-Image

OpenAI's DALL-E generates images from text descriptions. CLIP learns visual concepts from internet text+image pairs without labels. Together they launch the text-to-image revolution that produces Stable Diffusion, Midjourney, and Imagen.

Vision Generative Multimodal
2021
AlphaFold 2 — Protein Folding Solved

DeepMind's AlphaFold 2 predicts protein structures with atomic accuracy — solving a 50-year grand challenge of biology. Science magazine names it the "Breakthrough of the Year." Over 200 million protein structures predicted and freely released.

Science Neural Milestone
2022
Stable Diffusion & Midjourney Go Public

Stability AI releases Stable Diffusion as open-source; Midjourney opens public beta. Text-to-image generation becomes accessible to millions overnight, triggering debates about art, copyright, and creative AI that continue today.

Vision Generative
Nov 2022
ChatGPT — AI Reaches the Mainstream

OpenAI launches ChatGPT, reaching 1 million users in 5 days and 100 million in 2 months — the fastest-growing consumer product in history. Every major tech company accelerates AI development. AI becomes a mainstream technology and cultural phenomenon.

LLM Milestone Cultural
2023
GPT-4, Claude, Gemini — The LLM Race

OpenAI releases GPT-4 (multimodal); Anthropic releases Claude; Google releases Gemini. Llama 2 from Meta democratises open-source LLMs. The "AI arms race" reshapes the global tech industry. Governments begin drafting AI regulation.

LLM Multimodal Policy
2024
Reasoning Models & Agents

OpenAI's o1 demonstrates chain-of-thought reasoning competitive with PhD-level scientists. AI agents capable of multi-step browser and computer use emerge. Sora generates cinematic video from text. AI begins replacing knowledge workers in targeted domains.

LLM Agents Reasoning
