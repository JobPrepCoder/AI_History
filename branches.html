Taxonomy of AI

AI Branches & Disciplines

From the single root planted in 1943, AI has grown into 24 distinct disciplines, each with its own history, methods, and impact on the world.

🧩
Born 1956 — Dartmouth
Symbolic AI

The original AI paradigm — representing knowledge as explicit rules, logic, and symbols. Dominated AI from 1956 to the late 1980s. Parent of expert systems, knowledge graphs, and ontologies.

Reasoning Foundational
Key milestones
1956Logic Theorist (Newell & Simon)
1958Lisp language invented
1965DENDRAL — first expert system
🧠
Born 1943 — McCulloch & Pitts
Neural Networks

Brain-inspired computing using layers of interconnected nodes. The foundational idea from 1943 evolved through the perceptron (1957), backprop (1986), deep learning (2006), and today's trillion-parameter models.

Learning Foundational
Key milestones
1943McCulloch-Pitts neuron model
1986Backpropagation popularised
2012AlexNet — deep learning breaks out
🎯
Born 1959 — Arthur Samuel
Machine Learning

Systems that improve from experience without being explicitly programmed. Encompasses supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised learning across decision trees, SVMs, random forests, and neural approaches.

Learning
Key milestones
1959Samuel coins "Machine Learning"
1992SVMs introduced by Vapnik
2001Random Forests — Breiman
🌊
Born 2006 — Hinton et al.
Deep Learning

Multi-layer neural networks trained on massive data using GPUs. The dominant paradigm since 2012. Sub-fields: CNNs for vision, RNNs/Transformers for sequences, autoencoders for generation.

Learning
2006Deep Belief Networks — Hinton
2012AlexNet wins ImageNet
2017Transformer architecture
🗣️
Born 1954 — Georgetown Experiment
Natural Language Processing

Teaching machines to read, write, and understand human language. Evolved from rules and grammars → statistical models → word embeddings → Transformers and LLMs that generate fluent text across all languages.

Language
1954Georgetown MT experiment
2013Word2Vec — neural word embeddings
2018BERT — bidirectional transformers
👁️
Born 1966 — MIT Summer Vision
Computer Vision

Enabling machines to see and understand images and video. From edge detection and Hough transforms to CNNs, object detection (YOLO, Faster R-CNN), semantic segmentation, and diffusion-based image synthesis.

Perception
1966MIT Summer Vision Project
1989LeNet — first practical CNN
2012AlexNet revolutionises vision
🎮
Born 1950s — Bellman/Samuel
Reinforcement Learning

Agents learn optimal behaviour through trial-and-error interaction with an environment, maximising cumulative reward. From Samuel's checkers (1959) to AlphaGo (2016) to ChatGPT's RLHF training (2022).

Learning Action
1990TD-Gammon master backgammon
2013DQN — Atari from pixels
2016AlphaGo defeats world champion
🦾
Born 1961 — Unimate
Robotics & Autonomous Systems

Physical AI systems that perceive and act in the real world. Combines vision, planning, manipulation, and locomotion. From factory arms to autonomous vehicles, Boston Dynamics, and humanoid robots.

Action Perception
1961Unimate — GM factory robot
2005Stanley wins DARPA Grand Challenge
2021Boston Dynamics Atlas parkour
📋
Born 1965 — DENDRAL
Expert Systems

Knowledge-based programs that emulate the decision-making of human experts using IF-THEN rules and inference engines. Commercially dominant in the 1980s. Evolved into knowledge graphs and Bayesian networks.

Reasoning
1965DENDRAL — chemistry
1972MYCIN — medical diagnosis
1982XCON saves DEC $40M/year
🎤
Born 1952 — Bell Labs Audrey
Speech Recognition & Synthesis

Converting spoken language to text (ASR) and text to speech (TTS). From rule-based phoneme models to HMMs to deep learning approaches used by Alexa, Siri, Google Assistant, and Whisper.

Perception Language
1952Bell Labs "Audrey" — digits only
1987Dragon Dictate commercial product
2022OpenAI Whisper — multilingual
✨
Born 2014 — GANs
Generative AI

Models that create new content — images, text, audio, video, code, 3D. Includes GANs, VAEs, diffusion models, and autoregressive LLMs. The fastest growing AI subfield 2020–present.

Generation
2014GANs — Goodfellow et al.
2021DALL-E — text-to-image
2022Stable Diffusion open-sourced
💬
Born 2018 — GPT / BERT
Large Language Models

Massive transformer models trained on internet-scale text. The central technology of the 2020s AI wave. GPT-3/4, Claude, Gemini, Llama, and others demonstrate emergent reasoning, coding, and creative capabilities.

Language Generation
2018GPT-1 and BERT
2020GPT-3 — 175B parameters
2022ChatGPT — mainstream AI
🗺️
Born 1960s — Semantic Networks
Knowledge Representation

Encoding world knowledge in machine-readable form. From semantic networks and frames to ontologies (OWL, RDF), knowledge graphs (Google, Wikidata), and neural knowledge stores.

Reasoning
1969Semantic networks — Quillian
1984CYC project — common sense
2012Google Knowledge Graph
🔍
Born 1957 — GPS
Planning & Search

Algorithms that find solutions by exploring state spaces. A* search, MCTS, SAT solvers, STRIPS planning. Core to game AI, route planning, logistics optimisation, and robotic task planning.

Reasoning Action
1957GPS — means-ends analysis
1968A* search algorithm
1997Deep Blue — minimax search
🐝
Born 1980s — Distributed AI
Multi-Agent Systems

Multiple AI agents interacting, cooperating, or competing to achieve goals. Applications in simulation, markets, autonomous fleets, and the emerging field of "AI agentic frameworks" (AutoGPT, CrewAI, LangGraph).

Action
1987Brooks — subsumption architecture
2004RoboCup — multi-agent soccer
2023GPT-4 powered AI agents
📊
Born 1763 — Thomas Bayes
Probabilistic & Bayesian AI

Reasoning under uncertainty using probability theory and Bayesian inference. Bayesian networks, hidden Markov models, Gaussian processes. Foundation of many ML algorithms and the brain's cognitive framework.

Reasoning Learning
1988Judea Pearl — Bayesian networks
1997HMMs dominate speech recognition
2001Bayesian spam filters
🧬
Born 1950s — Holland
Evolutionary Computation

Optimisation algorithms inspired by biological evolution: genetic algorithms, evolutionary strategies, neuroevolution. Used in engineering design, neural architecture search, and game AI (OpenAI Five used ES).

Learning
1960Genetic algorithms — Holland
2017OpenAI Evolution Strategies
2019NEAT neuroevolution
🛡️
Born 2000s — Bostrom / MIRI
AI Safety & Alignment

Ensuring AI systems are safe, beneficial, and aligned with human values. Covers interpretability, robustness, RLHF, Constitutional AI, red-teaming, and long-term existential risk research. Fastest-growing AI research area 2020–present.

Safety
2014Nick Bostrom: "Superintelligence"
2017Anthropic / OpenAI safety teams
2022RLHF — ChatGPT alignment
🌐
Born 2021 — CLIP / DALL-E
Multimodal AI

AI systems that understand and generate across multiple modalities: text, images, audio, video, and code. GPT-4V, Gemini Ultra, Claude 3, LLaVA, and Sora represent the current frontier of multimodal intelligence.

Generation Perception
2021CLIP + DALL-E launch
2023GPT-4 Vision released
2024Sora — video generation
🔬
Born 1980s — DENDRAL, later AlphaFold
AI for Science

Applying AI to accelerate scientific discovery. AlphaFold solved protein folding; ML models accelerate drug discovery, materials science, climate modelling, particle physics, and mathematics.

Science Learning
2020AlphaFold 1 — protein structures
2021AlphaFold 2 — all known proteins
2023AI discovers new antibiotics
⚖️
Born 1980s, formalised 2010s
AI Ethics & Fairness

Studying and mitigating bias, discrimination, and social harms in AI systems. Covers fairness metrics, privacy, transparency, explainability, and the societal impact of automated decision-making at scale.

Ethics
2016ProPublica COMPAS bias report
2018Timnit Gebru — facial recognition bias
2023EU AI Act — first major AI law
💡
Born 1980s — Carver Mead
Neuromorphic Computing

Hardware architectures that mimic the brain's neural structure for ultra-low-power AI. Intel's Loihi, IBM's TrueNorth, and SpiNNaker implement spiking neural networks in silicon for edge AI applications.

Hardware Learning
1989Carver Mead coins "neuromorphic"
2014IBM TrueNorth — 1M neurons
2018Intel Loihi — spiking NN chip
🏥
Born 1972 — MYCIN
AI in Healthcare

Medical diagnosis, drug discovery, medical imaging, genomics, and patient monitoring. AI now matches radiologists on chest X-ray diagnosis and ophthalmologists on diabetic retinopathy screening.

Applied Perception
1972MYCIN diagnostic system
2016Google AI beats radiologists
2021AlphaFold + drug discovery
⚛️
Emerging — 2010s onwards
Quantum Machine Learning

Leveraging quantum computing to accelerate or enhance machine learning algorithms. Quantum neural networks, variational quantum circuits, and quantum annealing for optimisation problems. Currently pre-commercial but intensely researched.

Emerging Learning
2013D-Wave quantum annealer
2019Google quantum supremacy claim
2023IBM 1000-qubit Condor chip
